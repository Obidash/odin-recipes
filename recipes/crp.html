Cinnamon Roll Pancakes

Description

This recipe will take you through some steps to make some super delicious vegan cinnamon roll pancakes! If you have ever wanted some fluffly, mouth wateringly tasting, and aesthetically appealing pancakes, well then you're in for a treat!

This recipe makes 12 big pancakes, so there's plenty to go around. Simply finish with an indulgent drizzle of icing sugar and maple syrup, and enjoy the culinary experience of a lifetime.

Hope you're ready now let's dive in!

Ingredients

Pancake Mix

  • 500g plain flour
  • 2 tbsp baking powder
  • 2 tsp salt
  • 2 tsp vanilla essence
  • 1 litre unsweetened almond milk
  • dairy-free butter, for frying

Cinnamon Mix

  • 120g dairy-free butter
  • 190g light brown sugar
  • 10g ground cinnamon

Maple Glaze Icing

  • 300g icing sugar
  • 50ml maple syrup, plus more for drizzling
  • 40ml water

Steps

  1. Preheat the oven to 50c. Put the flour, baking powder, salt, vanilla essence and almond milk into a bowl and whisk together. Pour into a jug.
  2. To make the cinnamon mix, put the butter in the microwave for a few seconds to melt (or melt in a small pan on a medium heat). Pour the melted butter into a bowl along with the sugar and cinnamon and stir to combine. Leave to cool and then transfer to a squeezy bottle or piping bag.
  3. Put the ingredients for the icing into a bowl and beat until smooth.
  4. Now cook your pancakes! Place the saucepan on a medium-high heat. Add ½ teaspoon of dairy-free butter and allow it to melt. Use a piece of kitchen paper to spread the melted butter around so the pan is fully greased. When the pan is really hot, pour half a cup or a small ladle of pancake mix into the centre.
  5. Quickly grab your cinnamon mix and squeeze an even, steady swirl from the centre of the pancake spiraling out to the edge. Cook for about 3-4 minutes, during which time bubbles with appear on the top. Lift up the edge of the pancake with a spatula to check if it’s cooked underneath – if it feels dry and looks golden brown, it’s ready.
  6. Flip and cook the other side for 1 minute, until it is perfectly golden brown.
  7. Put the pancake on a plate, cover it with a tea towel and put it in the oven. Repeat to make all the pancakes. Remove from the oven and stack ‘em high on a plate. Drizzle artfully with the icing, then pour over lashings of maple syrup and serve.
